Tamarind Tree

Large evergreen tree, upto 50–60 feet tall, native to Africa and naturalised in India
Produces sour brown pods with tangy pulp, used extensively in cooking
The tree has a broad, spreading canopy, offering good shade
It has medicinal uses include aiding digestion
Its wood is often used as firewood
The word “tamarind” comes from an Arabic term “tamar-a-hind”, which means “Indian date"
