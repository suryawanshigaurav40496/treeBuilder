Raintree

Large, spreading tree, Upto 80-250 feet tall, native to Central and South America
The leaves fold in rainy weather and in the evenings, hence the names rain tree and five o'clock tree
Produces round, powder puff-shaped flowers, typically pink and white in colour
Known for providing significant shade with its wide canopy
Rain tree can tolerate short periods of drought, but they don't do well during long droughts
The wide canopy of the rain tree, with its intricate branching patterns, resembles the formations of a thunderstorm
